⚔️ Technology Comparison

MiniLED Technology - The Game Changer

✨ Picture Quality

Near-OLED contrast with superior brightness. Peak brightness can exceed 2000 nits.

💰 Cost Advantage

40-50% cheaper to produce than OLED at similar sizes. Uses existing LCD infrastructure.

📏 Size Flexibility

Easily scalable to 85"+ without exponential cost increases.

🏭 Manufacturing

Chinese companies control 70% of global LCD capacity, enabling competitive pricing.

OLED Technology - Premium Under Pressure

🎨 Picture Perfection

Perfect blacks, infinite contrast ratio. Still the gold standard for picture quality.

💸 High Costs

Manufacturing complexity keeps prices high. Limited suppliers (LG Display, Samsung Display).

📐 Size Limitations

Costs increase dramatically above 65". 77" OLED costs 2-3x more than equivalent MiniLED.

📉 Margin Pressure

ASP erosion of 15-20% YoY as MiniLED offers "good enough" quality at larger sizes.

Future Technologies - What's Next?

💎 MicroLED

The ultimate display tech. Self-emissive like OLED but with LED durability. Commercial viability by 2027.

🤖 AI Processing

Advanced upscaling and picture enhancement. May become more important than panel technology.

🔲 QD-OLED

Samsung's hybrid approach. Combines quantum dots with OLED for enhanced color and brightness.

📱 Smart Integration

TV as smart home hub. Software ecosystem may drive purchasing more than display specs.

Strategic Implications

The TV industry is experiencing its most significant disruption since the flat-panel transition. Size has trumped quality as the primary purchase driver. Winners will be those who can deliver the largest screens at acceptable quality for the lowest price. The next 24 months will determine whether Samsung & LG can defend their premium positioning or if Chinese brands will permanently reset the industry's competitive dynamics.
